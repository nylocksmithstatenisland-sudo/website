When Should You Replace Your Locks?

🔑 Outdated Security

Older locks may use outdated technology that's easily picked or bypassed by modern criminals.

💔 Worn or Damaged

Locks that are worn, rusted, or damaged may not function properly or provide adequate security.

🔐 Lost or Stolen Keys

After losing keys or experiencing a break-in, replacing locks prevents unauthorized access.

🏠 Moving In/Out

When moving into a new property or renting out your home, fresh locks provide peace of mind.

🔒 Property Changes

Adding security features, changing door types, or upgrading to smart locks requires new hardware.

📈 Enhanced Security

Upgrading to high-security locks provides better protection against break-ins and theft.

Should I replace the entire lock or just the cylinder for my Staten Island property?

The decision to replace the entire lock or just the cylinder depends on several factors. If the lock body is in good condition and you only need to change the keys (e.g., after losing keys or moving in), rekeying the cylinder is often sufficient and more cost-effective. However, if the lock is damaged, outdated, or you want to upgrade to a higher security level or a different type of lock, replacing the entire unit is recommended. We provide expert advice to help you make the best choice for your Staten Island home or business. See our Rekey Service and Lock Replacement services for more information.
