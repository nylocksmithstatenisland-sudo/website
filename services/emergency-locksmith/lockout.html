How to Prevent Future Lockouts

While we're always here when you need us, these practical tips can help you avoid lockout situations in the future.

Spare Key Solutions

  • • Give a spare house key to a trusted neighbor or family member
  • • Consider a keyless smart lock with backup codes
  • • Keep a spare car key in your wallet (not in the car!)
  • • Use a magnetic key box hidden on your vehicle's undercarriage

Daily Habits

  • • Develop a routine of checking for keys before closing doors
  • • Always lock your car using the key fob, not the interior button
  • • Keep keys on a bright, easy-to-spot keychain
  • • Set reminders on your phone for important keys

Technology Solutions

  • • Upgrade to keypad entry systems or smart locks
  • • Use Bluetooth key trackers (Tile, AirTag) on your keyring
  • • Install a video doorbell for remote access monitoring
  • • Set up auto-unlock features on compatible smart locks

Maintenance

  • • Maintain your locks to prevent jams and failures
  • • Replace worn keys before they break in the lock
  • • Test spare keys periodically to ensure they still work
  • • Consider rekeying locks when moving to a new home

Frequently Asked Questions About Lockouts

What should I do immediately after realizing I'm locked out?

First, stay calm and assess your safety. Move to a well-lit, safe location if you're outside. Check if any windows or alternate doors are unlocked, but don't attempt to break in yourself as this can cause injury or property damage. Call us at (718) 831-6269 for professional lockout assistance. We arrive within 20-30 minutes on average. While waiting, make sure you have identification and proof of ownership ready for verification.

Can you get me back in without breaking my lock?

Yes! We specialize in non-destructive entry methods. Our trained technicians use specialized lockout tools like lock picks, slim jims, wedges, and bypass tools to gain entry without damaging your locks, doors, or vehicle. Over 95% of our lockout jobs are completed without any damage. In rare cases where a lock is severely damaged or a high-security situation requires it, we'll discuss all options and get your approval before proceeding with any destructive methods.

How quickly can you respond to a lockout in Staten Island?

Our average response time is 20-30 minutes across Staten Island. We strategically position our mobile locksmith units throughout the borough in neighborhoods like St. George, Great Kills, Tottenville, New Dorp, and Port Richmond to ensure rapid arrival times. Response times may vary slightly depending on your exact location, current traffic conditions, and time of day. When you call, our dispatcher will provide an accurate ETA for your specific situation.

What if I'm locked out with children or pets inside?

This is an urgent priority situation. Call 911 immediately first, especially if it's hot weather or a child or pet appears distressed. Then call us at (718) 831-6269—we'll expedite our response and may coordinate with emergency services if needed. Your child's or pet's safety is paramount, and emergency responders may need to break a window for immediate access in extreme situations. We'll work as quickly as possible to provide safe entry.

What lockout tools and techniques do you use?

We use professional-grade lockout tools designed specifically for non-destructive entry. For residential lockouts, we employ lock picks, bump keys, plug spinners, and bypass tools. For automotive lockouts, we use slim jims, long reach tools, air wedges, and specialized automotive entry kits that won't damage paint or weather stripping. For commercial properties, we have tools for high-security locks, panic bars, and electronic access systems. All our technicians are trained in proper tool usage to maximize success while minimizing any risk of damage.

Can you help if my key broke off in the lock during a lockout?

Yes! Broken key extraction is a common scenario we handle during lockout situations. Our technicians carry specialized extraction tools to safely remove broken key fragments from locks without damaging the lock cylinder. After extraction, we can cut a replacement key on-site from the broken pieces or by decoding the lock. If the lock has been damaged by the break, we can also perform lock repair or replacement as needed.
